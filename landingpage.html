Mirrorless, high-end cameras

Powerful generation

The Sony a6000 offers a major change related to the sensor, the new 24.3 megapixel Exmor APS HD CMOS sensor has on-chip phase detection like its predecessor but covers a much larger portion of the frame. Sony promises better AF tracking, especially when shooting continuously. The a6000 uses Sony's latest image processor, Bionz X, which boasts with improved detail and smarter noise reduciton.

No more NEX

The a6000 sits in the same place as Sony's mirrorless lineup and offers broadly the same features, but also adds a number of significant features while loosing the NEX moniker. The resolution and processor have been bumped up and the most noteable feature of the a6000 is its updated autofocus-system.

Key features

  • 24.3 megapixel APS-C CMOS sensor
  • Bionz X image processor
  • Hybrid AF system with 25 contras-detect and 179 phase-detect points
  • Built-in flash + Multi-interface shoe
  • 11 fps continous shooting with subject-tracking
  • 3-inch tilting LCD with 921.600 dots
  • OLED electronic viewfiender @ 1.44M dots
  • Diffraction correction, area-specific noise reduction and detail reporoduction technology
  • Full HD video recording @ 1080/60p and 24p
  • Wi-Fi and NFC capability, for transfering images directly to your phone or laptop
